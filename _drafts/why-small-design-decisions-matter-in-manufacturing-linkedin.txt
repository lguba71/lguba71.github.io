Small design decisions often look insignificant in CAD.

A hole position.
A chamfer.
A radius.
A tolerance.
A fastener direction.
A surface finish note.

But in manufacturing, inspection, assembly and maintenance, these details can create very real effects.

A small design choice can decide whether a part is easy to fixture or difficult to locate.

It can decide whether a standard tool can be used or a special machining strategy is needed.

It can decide whether assembly is straightforward or uncomfortable for the operator.

Manufacturing-focused mechanical design is not only about the main concept.

It is also about the small decisions that reduce friction later in the process.
